Mulvium’s Vision

Beauty should be all around us.

21st Century Patronage

Building castles. Reclaiming beauty.

Mulvium is a non-profit foundation that funnels personal wealth into global impact— incubating the creative process, commissioning master craftsmen, and restoring aspiration to public life.

Three Constituencies

The Patron

Mulvium aligns the Overton window in favor of accumulated wealth powering the frontier, which should be inexorably tied to artistic achievement.

The Artist

Mulvium redirects artistic creativity toward problem solving rather than exclusively self-expression—through polymathy and humanist-technologist collaboration.

The Citizen

Through art and technology, Mulvium will manifest and maintain the framework of social humility society needs to reconcile divisiveness and engender harmony.

Meet the Divisions

01

International Festival Orchestra

A travelling Kappellmeister-led ensemble writing compositions for regular service— in the tradition of J.S. Bach as Konzertmeister—so that beauty can be all around us.

02

Castles

Modular civic landmarks that host political forums, theater, and educational summits. Fine artists are commissioned to design and adorn them with frescos, mosaics, reliefs, and free-standing sculpture.

03

Education

Wielding technology in the pursuit of perfect polymathy—wandering freely and with unbridled speed through all the fields of the disciplines, as Johannes Wower defined it in 1603.

04

Economic Zones

Castles as piazze: tolls, small businesses, and a B2C suite that empowers scholars, artists, and independent thinkers to co-create a technological system intelligible, pluralistic, and beneficial to humanity.

The Vision

Most things that are profoundly beautiful are wrapped up in the canon of the past and in the splendor of the natural world. Why can’t we find this level of beauty in our globalized, cosmopolitan lives—in the vast art of our time?

Historically, societies made their deepest values visible. Medieval Europe externalized faith through cathedrals. Renaissance Italy materialized influence through palazzi, frescoes, and sculpture. Even industrial modernity expressed itself through monumental infrastructure—bridges and train stations—that were not merely functional, but aspirational.

Today, the political and technological classes obfuscate beauty more than shape it. There needs to exist a funnel where personal wealth comes in and global impact comes out— where creative ideation and the creative process can be incubated.

If people can’t tolerate aesthetic differences, how will people tolerate each other?

Two-Tier Model

Tier I

Non-Profit Foundation

Directly controls real estate, castle operations, and brand. This is where patrons contribute their wealth.

Tier II

Public Benefit Corporation

A subsidiary controlled by the non-profit board and other investors. This arm develops technology and media.

Mulvium’s hope is to help the world stop and smell the roses—one artwork at a time.
