Proportio Prima — The Proposal

The Kitezh Festival Orchestra is a proposed international ensemble in residence in Cambridge, Massachusetts. Given the recent transformation of Boston’s cultural scene, there is a new need for a galvanizing musical force that is mission-focused, globally-oriented, and programmatically courageous.

Dupla — The Festival

Kitezh will present a week-long series of concerts, talks, and world premieres every September — all tackling the complex meaning and importance of aesthetic beauty. Every year will have a different theme.

Tripla — Anno MMXXVII

In September 2027, the festival will memorialize the 200th anniversary of Beethoven’s passing. It will be bookended by the Eroica Symphony and the Missa Solemnis — the craftsmanship and the transcendence.

Quadrupla — Reimaginings

Kitezh also features Reimaginings: classical and non-classical composers extend the symphonic genre by composing an original symphonic movement or tone poem that incorporates multimedia, different genres, voice, choreography, and cinema. We need art that intertwines craftsmanship in form with depth in content — akin to Beethoven.

Quintupla — The Ensemble

The orchestra will feature world-class instrumentalists across generations and backgrounds — a travelling Kappellmeister-led ensemble writing compositions for regular service, in the tradition of J. S. Bach as Konzertmeister, so that beauty can be all around us.

Sextupla — First Reclamation

This non-profit festival is the first reclamation of the beautiful. Yet this attention to beauty must extend beyond live music. The end goal is to reshape patronage in the arts. Contemporary art funded today — especially the entertainment–industrial complex — largely overlooks beauty as a source of social cohesion.

Septupla — The Sublime

People need art that inspires deep feelings. Great art inspires the spiritual, the sublime, and the transcendent within us.

Octupla — The Founding

The first step to reshaping arts patronage is to produce and sustain this festival orchestra. An initial funding round will enable the first hires — Executive Director, Artistic Producer, Orchestra Manager, principal musicians, and the commissioned composers of Reimaginings — alongside the creation of a Board of Advisors: artistic luminaries and patrons who affirm the legitimacy of the operation and attract top talent.

—   The Myth of Kitezh   —

The original myth describes a thirteenth-century Russian city that submerged in a lake following a Mongol invasion. The city remains invisible, but people still can hear its celestial carillons and canticles.

This is an invitation to join as a founding member of the Board of Advisors, and to make a philanthropic contribution toward the first reclamation of the beautiful.

— Benjamin T. Rossen, Artistic Director
